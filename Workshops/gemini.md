# **Workshop Plan: Unlocking Team Productivity with Gemini**

**Target Audience:** Up to 30 Professional Team Members (Mixed AI Literacy).
**Duration:** 90 Minutes.
**Theme:** "From Zero to Gemini Hero: Leveraging AI for Daily Impact."

---

## **Part 1: Introduction - The New AI Playbook (15 Minutes)**

The goal: De-mystify LLMs and clearly position Gemini as a professional tool.

### **1. Welcome & The Big Picture (5 min)**

* **The Hook:** Start with a quick poll (hand raise or chat): "What's the most time-consuming, repetitive task you did last week? Tell me in one word."
* **The LLM Analogy:** Introduce **Generative AI** and **LLMs** (Large Language Models). Explain that Gemini is not just a search engine; think of it as a **Hyper-Intelligent Intern** or a **Creative Co-Pilot**. It doesn't just retrieve; it *creates* and *reasons*.

### **2. Meet Gemini: Unique Strengths (10 min)**

Highlight these unique features to show the model's distinct value:

* **Native Multimodality:** Gemini was built to understand more than just text. It can process, reason, and generate responses based on **text, images, and code** simultaneously.
    * *Analogy:* It's fluent in several languages (text, visuals, logic) at once.
* **Advanced Reasoning and Code:** It excels at complex, multi-step tasks that require logic, planning, and problem-solving, making it highly capable at **generating, explaining, and debugging code**.
* **Google Ecosystem Integration (if applicable):** Mention its powerful connection to services like **Gmail, Docs, and Sheets** (if your team has Gemini for Workspace access). This shows direct professional utility.

---

## **Part 2: Demonstration - The Power of the Prompt (20 Minutes)**

Emphasize that **the quality of the output depends on the quality of the input.** Use a shared screen for these live demos.

| Prompt Theme | Focus Skill | Sample Prompt (You Demonstrate) | Key Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Productivity & Role** | **Persona & Output Structuring** | "You are an experienced Project Manager. Summarize the below meeting transcript \[paste a short bulleted list] into 3 bullet points: a **Decision**, a **Risk**, and an **Action Item with owner**." | Defines a **Role**, provides **Context**, and sets a clear **Format Constraint**. |
| **Creative Problem Solving** | **Tone & Context Shifting** | "We need to communicate a new policy about \[Insert a slightly boring policy idea]. Rewrite this policy as a friendly, engaging, and enthusiastic 4-paragraph internal blog post." | Forces a **Creative Shift** from dry text to engaging content. |
| **Technical Assistance** | **Code/Logic Reasoning** | "I need a formula for Google Sheets that will check if the date in cell A2 is a Monday AND if the value in cell B2 is greater than 100. Write the formula and explain what each part does." | Leverages **Technical Proficiency** and requires **Step-by-Step Explanation**. |
| **Multimodal Magic** | **Visual Analysis** | *\[Upload a picture of a complex diagram, chart, or whiteboard sketch].* "Analyze this image. What are the three main components of the system shown, and what is the likely next step we should take to complete the design?" | Demonstrates its ability to **reason from visual input**. |

## **Part 3: Hands-On Challenge - "The AI Prompt Game" (55 Minutes)**

This game focuses on achieving specific, constrained outcomes—the core of effective professional prompting.

### **Game Setup:**

* Divide into **small teams of 3-4**.
* Each team needs one laptop/user logged into **Gemini**.
* **Scoring:** Teams get points for completing the tasks *most accurately* and *most creatively/efficiently*. The winner gets bragging rights (and maybe coffee).

### **The Game: The Triple-Constraint Challenge**

The goal is to solve a common professional challenge using Gemini, hitting three simultaneous constraints in the output.

| Round | Time | The Challenge (Topic) | Triple Constraints to Hit (The Scorecard) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. The Summarizer** | 15 min | **Topic:** Summarize a lengthy (provided) email thread or document (paste 2-3 paragraphs of dense text). | **1.** Must be **less than 100 words**. **2.** Must be written in the voice of a **CEO/Executive**. **3.** Must include **2 bolded action items**. |
| **2. The Recruiter** | 15 min | **Topic:** Draft a job description for a new role on your team (e.g., "AI Prompt Engineer" or "Junior Data Analyst"). | **1.** Must be formatted as a **3-column markdown table**. **2.** Must explicitly mention **3 soft skills** (e.g., collaboration, empathy). **3.** The required experience level must be set to **"Entry Level."** |
| **3. The Educator** | 15 min | **Topic:** Explain a concept central to your professional field (e.g., "Agile Methodology" or "Quarterly Budgeting"). | **1.** Must use a **sports analogy** (e.g., baseball, rugby). **2.** Must be written at a **6th-grade reading level**. **3.** Must end with a **question** to the reader. |
| **Wrap-up & Debrief** | 10 min | Review the winning prompts. Discuss the strategy. | **Key Takeaway:** The "secret" is in the details. **Context, Persona, Format, and Constraint** are the four pillars of a great prompt. |

---

## **Post-Workshop & Next Steps**

1.  **Check-in:** Ask the team for a one-word feeling about using AI now (e.g., "Excited," "Comfortable," "Curious").
2.  **The Homework Prompt:** Give them a useful prompt to try out *tomorrow*:
    > "Draft a daily to-do list for me. Organize it by priority (High, Medium, Low) and include a 15-minute block for 'AI Experimentation' in the schedule. Base the list on the three most common tasks of my job."
3.  **Final Statement:** Reiterate that AI is a **tool**, not a replacement. Increasing their literacy and comfort means they are better equipped to leverage the world's most powerful new co-pilot.
